## Post

€5.88 billion in GDPR fines since 2018.

TikTok: €530M. Meta: €479M.

And the rules just changed again: regulators are no longer waiting for complaints. They're proactively testing websites themselves — scanning for consent violations, pixel misuse, and unlawful data transfers.

Most businesses still treat data compliance as a legal problem, not a marketing problem.

That framing is outdated.

Your ad pixels, your analytics, your consent banners — these are the front lines of regulatory risk now. A compliance failure isn't just a fine. It's public exposure and brand damage at the same moment your customers are deciding whether to trust you with their data.

The brands that built clean data practices from the start aren't just avoiding fines.

They're earning trust in a market where trust is increasingly the product.
