## Post

Meta just changed how it attributes conversions.

If you're running ads, this affects your numbers.

Here's what changed:

Click-through attribution now only counts actual link clicks. Non-link interactions — swipes, profile taps, expanding the post — are now categorized separately as "engage-through" attribution.

Video engaged-view threshold dropped from 10 seconds to 5. Meta's data shows most conversions happen faster than previously measured.

What this means for your reports: your click-through numbers may look lower, your video attribution may look higher.

Neither is a bug. It's Meta getting more precise about what actually drove the conversion.

If your ROAS shifted recently and nothing in your campaigns changed, check your attribution model before you start adjusting budgets.

Understanding what counts as a "conversion" in your attribution window is the most underrated skill in performance marketing.
