## Post

60 to 70% of SaaS churn happens in the first 90 days.

Most companies read that and think "onboarding problem."

It's not.

It's a promise problem.

By the time a customer cancels in week 8, something broke in week 1 — or before the sale even closed. A feature was implied that didn't exist. An integration assumed that wasn't there. A workflow described in the demo that the product couldn't actually deliver.

The gap between what you sell and what you ship is where churn lives.

The companies with the best retention don't win because they have the best email sequences or in-app tooltips.

They win because what they promised in the sales and marketing process is exactly what the product delivers.

Retention strategy starts there — not in the customer success playbook.
