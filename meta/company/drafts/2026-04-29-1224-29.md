## Post

Meta changed how ad attribution works in 2026 — and most advertisers are comparing Q2 numbers to benchmarks that used a different methodology.

Here's what changed:

Video views now count at 5 seconds, not 10. Your view counts look higher and cost-per-view looks lower. It doesn't mean your video got better.

Link clicks no longer include likes or shares on the same post. If link click volume dropped, this is part of why.

Engage-through attribution replaced engaged-view. How Meta tracks someone who sees your ad and converts later has changed. Your attribution window data changed with it.

None of this automatically means your campaigns are underperforming.

It means the ruler changed. The brands that don't know that will make bad optimization decisions — cutting campaigns that are working, scaling ones that aren't, and spending Q2 budget solving a problem that wasn't there.

Before you make changes based on this quarter's numbers, verify what you're actually comparing against.
