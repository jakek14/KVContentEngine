## Post

CCPA changed the rules on January 1, 2026.

Your "Accept" button can't be bigger, brighter, or easier to click than "Decline." Consent through inaction is no longer valid. Pre-checked boxes are out.

Most consent flows weren't built to this spec. Which means most are non-compliant right now.

19 states are sharing enforcement data. This isn't a gray area anymore — it's a direct regulatory requirement with coordinated enforcement behind it.

Audit your cookie banners before a regulator does it for you.

## Context

CCPA's expanded consent rules took effect January 1, 2026, introducing symmetry requirements for accept/decline buttons, banning consent-by-inaction, and removing pre-checked defaults. These are specific, enforceable requirements — not guidance. And multi-state enforcement coordination means a violation in California could trigger scrutiny in 18 other states.
