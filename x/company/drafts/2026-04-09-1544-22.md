## Post

New CCPA rules took effect January 1, 2026.

Most brands still haven't read them.

Here's what changed:

Closing or navigating away from a cookie consent banner no longer counts as consent. Not legally, not in California. If the user didn't click "Accept," you don't have consent — no matter what your banner says.

The opt-out path now has to be as easy as the opt-in. Same number of steps. No more hiding the "No Thanks" button.

CalPrivacy issued its first enforcement action of the year in March. $1.1 million fine.

The brands building clean data practices from the start aren't just avoiding fines.

They're earning something harder to manufacture: a customer base that trusts them with their data.
