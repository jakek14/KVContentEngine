The privacy enforcement window is closed.

California: $7,988 per intentional violation. Cure period: gone.

Montana: Right-to-cure expired April 1.

8 states now require Global Privacy Control support.

"We'll fix it when we get a notice" is no longer a strategy.

## Context

Multiple state privacy enforcement milestones converged in April 2026. California's CPRA raised fines to $7,988 per intentional violation and eliminated the 30-day automatic cure period. Montana's right-to-cure period for its Consumer Data Privacy Act expired April 1, 2026. Eight states now require businesses to honor Global Privacy Control signals. This is distinct from the CCPA consent banner post on April 9 (which covered January 2026 rule changes) — this covers the enforcement posture shift: no more grace periods, immediate enforcement across multiple states.
