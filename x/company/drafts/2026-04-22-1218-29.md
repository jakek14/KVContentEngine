## Post

Stores without an owned data strategy are paying 40–60% more for customer acquisition than they were five years ago.

Not because ads got more expensive.

Because they lost access to the signals that made targeting work.

Third-party cookies. IDFA. Cross-site tracking. All degraded or gone.

The brands that never built their own data layer are now bidding blind on platforms that know less about their customers than they used to.

The ones who invested in first-party data — who know who visited, who converted, who came back — are paying less and reaching more of the right people.

First-party data isn't a competitive advantage in 2026.

It's the baseline. If you don't have it, you're subsidizing the brands that do.

The visitors already on your site are the most valuable first-party signal you have.

Most companies still can't tell you who they were.
