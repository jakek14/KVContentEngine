## Post

Google reversed cookie deprecation.

It changes nothing.

The brands that spent 2024 and 2025 building first-party data strategies already adapted. They're not going back.

The ones celebrating the reprieve are still in the same spot they were two years ago — dependent on a signal that was already broken on Safari and Firefox, which together account for more than half of all web traffic.

Third-party cookies were never the answer. They were an approximation. Cross-device matching was always leaky. ITP had been eroding them for years.

The reversal isn't a green light.

It's a yellow one.

The winners already ran it.

## Context

Google's April 2025 announcement reversed its cookie deprecation plans, keeping third-party cookies in Chrome by default. However, experts widely note this doesn't change the broader trajectory — cookies are already blocked on Safari and Firefox (majority of non-Chrome traffic), and the brands that invested in first-party data strategies have no reason to backtrack. The reprieve mainly benefits companies that didn't adapt.
