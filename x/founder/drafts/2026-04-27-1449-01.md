## Post

Meta is projected to overtake Google in global ad revenue in 2026.

$243 billion vs $239 billion.

For the first time in the history of digital advertising, Google will not be the largest ad platform.

This deserves more attention than most founders are giving it.

Google built on intent — you search, you signal exactly what you want, they sell access to that signal.

Meta built on discovery — you weren't looking. The algorithm decided you might want it anyway.

The handoff is happening because intent-based discovery is fragmenting. People now research in AI tools, Perplexity, ChatGPT. Google's grip on the moment of search is loosening.

The social discovery layer is absorbing what Google is losing.

For founders: your growth playbook was built around certain ad platform economics. Those economics are shifting. This is the year to question assumptions you haven't revisited in three years.
