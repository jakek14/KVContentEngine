## Post

Fortune's right — trust is a real bottleneck in AI-assisted development.

But there's a more upstream problem they're pointing at without naming:

Most people don't know what they want to build precisely enough.

The AI executes whatever you give it.

Vague spec, vague output. Change your mind every three prompts, get a codebase that reflects that chaos. The trust issues downstream are usually spec issues upstream.

The founders shipping the most with AI tools aren't the ones who learned to trust the output blindly.

They're the ones who write the clearest briefs before they start.

Know what done looks like. Then hand it off.

That's the skill nobody's teaching.
