## Post

Jason nailed the problem.

The traditional SaaS exit playbook is under real pressure. Build to $20M ARR, show the growth rate, find a strategic acquirer — that path is harder than it was three years ago.

But here's the flip side that doesn't get talked about:

The companies sitting in the best position right now aren't chasing the old playbook. They're the ones who stopped building for an exit and started building for cash flow.

Profitable. Lean. Real product-market fit that doesn't require a deck to explain.

When you're not built to flip, a broken exit market doesn't threaten you. It reveals your edge.

https://x.com/jasonlk/status/1979640758157390281

## Context

Jason Lemkin has been vocal about the SaaS exit market tightening — strategic acquirers are pickier, PE rollups are slowing, and even strong companies can't find the clean exits that were easy in 2020-2021. This is a great moment to validate that take and add the counterpoint: bootstrapped/profitable founders are uniquely insulated from this shift.
