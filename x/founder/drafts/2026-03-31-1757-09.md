## Post

**Tweet 1**
CAC for B2B SaaS jumped 31% last year.

Not because you got worse at marketing.

Because the platforms you're buying on ran out of room.

**Tweet 2**
The math is brutal.

You're competing against thousands of SaaS companies in the same Google auction. Meta's algorithm is smarter — but so is every advertiser using it.

Platforms win. You pay more. Every year.

**Tweet 3**
The companies keeping CAC flat aren't "just doing paid better."

They shifted the question from "how do we get more traffic?" to "how do we extract more value from the traffic we already have?"

**Tweet 4**
Think about what you already own:

— Visitors who never filled out a form
— Email leads who never converted
— Retargeting audiences sitting idle

Most teams pour budget into acquisition and ignore the 97% of paid traffic that already bounced.

**Tweet 5**
The highest-ROI move in 2026 isn't a new channel.

It's getting more out of the spend you're already burning.

Know who visited. Follow up faster. Stop leaking paid traffic into a black hole.

**Tweet 6**
Paid media costs will keep rising.

You can spend more to keep pace.

Or you can make the traffic you're already buying work harder.

One of those is a treadmill. The other compounds.

## Context

A 2026 Profitwell report analyzing 14,800 companies confirmed CAC rose 31.2% year-over-year for B2B SaaS — the steepest single-year jump on record. Overall, CAC has climbed 60% over the past five years. The McElligott Digital Marketing 2025 Google Ads Benchmark Report independently confirms rising ad costs across paid channels heading into 2026.

The thread uses the free-game pillar: no pitch, just a real framework for thinking about acquisition efficiency. The insight is that paid media has become a commodity auction — every SaaS company is running the same plays on the same platforms. The actual edge in 2026 is capturing and converting the traffic you've already paid to attract.
