## Post

The average B2B company takes 47 hours to respond to a new inbound lead.

Best-in-class respond in 5 minutes.

Studies show the probability of qualifying a lead drops 80% after the first five minutes. After an hour, you've lost nearly all of it.

So most companies are generating inbound attention — spending real money on ads, SEO, content — and doing almost nothing useful with it.

The obvious fix looks like process. Faster reps. Better SLAs. Routing rules.

But there's a step before all of that.

Most website visitors don't fill out a form. They land, they look around, they leave. No name. No email. Just a session that bounced and a stat in your analytics.

You can't respond in 5 minutes to someone you have no record of.

The response speed problem and the visibility problem are the same problem.

That's why knowing who's on your site is a revenue operations issue, not just a data team issue.
