## Post

**Tweet 1**
Harvard just asked: does vibe coding offer a window into our AI future?

Worth reading. But I think founders should be asking a sharper question.

https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2026/04/vibe-coding-may-offer-insight-into-our-ai-future/

**Tweet 2**
Harvard's frame: anyone can now build software.

Democratization is real.

But when ANYONE can build it, software itself becomes a commodity.

So what doesn't get commoditized?

**Tweet 3**
The answer isn't speed — everyone gets faster.

The moat:
— Knowing which problem is actually worth solving
— Understanding your customer well enough to build the RIGHT thing
— Getting there before someone else does

Execution was the bottleneck.

Now it's judgment.

**Tweet 4**
The founders who scare me aren't the ones who ship fastest.

They're the ones who understand their market so clearly that even with infinite execution speed, competitors can't catch them.

They keep seeing problems everyone else misses.

**Tweet 5**
Vibe coding won't replace founders who see clearly.

It'll expose the ones who were hiding behind slow execution.
