## Post

Chamath nailed the hard part.

The 10x productivity gain for individuals who know how to use the tools — that's real.

But what follows isn't automatic.

Most founding teams are shipping faster with the same organizational structure they had before. Same coordination overhead. Same meeting cadences. Same approval loops.

The bottleneck just moved.

You're producing output faster, but the operating model hasn't changed to absorb it. Decisions still take the same time. Priorities still get muddled. Context still lives in too many places.

The founders building differently aren't just using better tools.

They're running smaller teams with higher individual leverage, cutting the coordination layer almost entirely, keeping raw signal from customers closer to the people making decisions.

That's the factory redesign.

The motor swap was the easy part.
