## Post

Chamath's right.

The SaaS companies under pressure right now aren't just facing a market correction.

They built on a premise that's now being tested: that growth, eventually, justifies everything.

It doesn't.

The companies not sweating this? They never needed growth to cover for bad unit economics. They solved a problem, charged for it, and kept costs below revenue.

Bootstrapping looks boring until the tide goes out.
