## Post

**Tweet 1**
The most expensive sales hire I see founders make is the first one.

Not because the rep was bad.

Because the founder didn't know what they were selling yet.

**Tweet 2**
When you do sales yourself, you hear the real objections.

Not the ones your deck anticipates — the ones that surface on call 12, from a prospect saying something completely unexpected.

That's the market talking.

That's the signal no hired rep can give you.

**Tweet 3**
The founders who hire a VP Sales at $500K MRR have one conversation.

The ones who wait until the pattern is clear — consistent ICP, repeatable close motion, objections they can counter cold — have a completely different one.

**Tweet 4**
The signal you need before hiring: you can predict outcomes.

X effort → Y pipeline → Z close rate.

If conversion still depends on you personally handling every edge case — you're not there.

No rep can replicate what hasn't been systematized yet.

**Tweet 5**
Founder-led sales isn't a phase to escape.

It's an education.

The ones who rushed out of it hired fast and lost the institutional knowledge about why customers actually buy.

The ones who sat in it long enough walked out with a playbook that works.

## Context

Pitchdrive, Dorian Barker, and David Cummings all document the founder-led sales trap — hiring a sales rep before the pattern is repeatable leads to underperformance, not scale. The counterintuitive take: staying in founder-led sales longer than feels comfortable is often the right call.
