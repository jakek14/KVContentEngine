## Post

Q1 2026: $297 billion raised in startup funding.

$122 billion of that went to one company.

The record-breaking quarter everyone is celebrating is mostly one deal.

Here's what that actually signals:

Venture capital didn't open up. It concentrated. The firms that have dry powder are making massive bets on a handful of AI infrastructure plays — and everything else is competing for the leftovers.

If you're building a vertical SaaS, a niche tool, a services-layer product — you're not in that funding environment. You're operating in a different market entirely.

That's not a bad thing.

The founders I respect most right now are the ones who looked at the AI mania, decided they weren't going to out-raise OpenAI, and went back to building something that generates actual revenue.

Record funding quarters don't mean the money flows downstream.

They usually mean it didn't.
