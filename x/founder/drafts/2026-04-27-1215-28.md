## Post

Seed investors in 2026 expect $300–500K ARR before they'll write a check.

Three years ago, that was the Series A bar.

The bar has moved so far that by the time you qualify for the round, you might not need the money.

Most founders use fundraising milestones as build targets.

"When I hit $X ARR, I can raise."

That's fine for tracking progress.

But the smarter question, asked early:

If I can get to $400K ARR without outside capital — what does a seed check actually buy me at that point?

Sometimes the answer is still yes. You want to hire faster, move into a new market, accelerate.

But sometimes the honest answer: you're trading equity and control for speed you don't actually need.

The seed round used to fund the search for product-market fit.

Now it comes after you've already solved the hardest unknowns.

Know what you're buying before you sign.
