## Post

He's right — and the implication for how you build is underrated.

The question isn't "how good is our product?"

It's: "how hard are we to rip out?"

Those feel like the same question. They're not.

Product quality wins trials.
Integration depth wins renewals.

In the AI era, any product can be rebuilt in a week by a skilled engineer with decent tools.

What can't be rebuilt is the data, the workflows, and the institutional muscle memory that forms around a tool people actually use every day.

The companies with the lowest churn aren't just shipping better features.

They're building more connections — to your CRM, your email stack, your analytics layer, your data warehouse.

Every integration is a moat.

Build accordingly.
