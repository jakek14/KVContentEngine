## Post

The per-seat model assumes users are the right proxy for value.

They're not.

Gartner: 70% of businesses will prefer usage-based pricing over per-seat by end of 2026.

Your pricing model is a positioning statement.

Build it around value delivered. Not headcount.

## Context

Gartner's 2026 data shows a massive shift away from per-seat toward usage/consumption-based pricing, accelerated by AI collapsing the connection between seat count and value created. When AI handles work that previously required multiple users, charging per seat becomes a proxy for the wrong thing.
