## Post

Bootstrapped founders keep ~70% at exit.

VC-backed founders: ~20%.

The math was always this clear. We just didn't look at it.

The pitch: raise more → grow faster → bigger outcome → everyone wins.

The reality: 5-7 dilutive rounds, pro-rata rights, preferences, and a liquidation stack that leaves most founders with a fraction of the headline number.

90% of bootstrapped companies that hit $1M in revenue are still operating 10 years later.

Most people focus on the size of the exit. The better question is how much of it you actually keep.

Pick your game deliberately. They're not the same game.
