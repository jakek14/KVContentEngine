## Post

Most founders price by looking at competitors.

That's the wrong input.

Your competitor's price tells you what they think their product is worth. Not what yours is worth.

The problem: you're inheriting someone else's positioning. Their costs. Their customers. Their ceiling.

Pricing is a statement. It signals where you sit, who you're for, and how seriously you take your own product.

Price too low and customers assume it's not that good.
Price high and you'll feel the pressure to justify every dollar — which usually makes the product better.

The shift in SaaS pricing right now: 43% of companies use hybrid models — a base fee plus variable usage or outcome-based components. That's projected to hit 61% by the end of the year.

Companies using outcome-based pricing see 31% higher customer retention and 21% higher satisfaction.

The reason isn't complicated: alignment. Customer pays more when they get more value. When incentives point the same direction, the relationship changes.

Most early-stage founders resist this because it feels complicated. But the complexity lives in your billing system — not in the customer's head.

Start simple. But don't start cheap.

## Context

SaaS pricing is a durable topic and hasn't been covered in recent posts. The research on hybrid and outcome-based pricing trends gives fresh data to anchor a founder take. Most founders underprice out of fear — this reframes pricing as a strategic signal, not just a revenue decision.
