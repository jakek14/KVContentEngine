## Post

**Tweet 1**
Meta's goal: by end of 2026, you submit a URL and a budget.

AI writes the creative, picks the audience, decides the placements, optimizes the spend.

That sounds like a feature.

It's also a trap.

**Tweet 2**
When Meta controls your creative, targeting, AND optimization — you can't learn anything.

Which creative drove the conversion? What audience actually bought?

You don't know.

The algorithm does.

**Tweet 3**
There's also a brand problem.

Meta's AI optimizes for Meta's metric. Usually clicks, or whatever conversion it's decided matters.

That's not the same as building a brand people remember when they're ready to buy.

**Tweet 4**
And a dependency problem.

65% of advertisers are now scaling through Advantage+.

The brands handing everything to the AI are one algorithm update away from their pipeline going quiet.

**Tweet 5**
The brands that win long-term aren't the ones with the best AI-generated Meta campaigns.

They're the ones building direct relationships with buyers that no platform controls.

The only edge that compounds.
