## Post

Your CRM has the wrong pipeline number.

Here's why:

It counts the deals people told you about.

Not the ones where someone spent 40 minutes on your pricing page this week.

Or opened your email sequence 6 times without replying.

Or came back to your site 3 times in 3 days.

None of that is in your CRM.

But all of it is intent.

The companies with the best-performing sales teams in 2026 aren't doing more outbound.

They're doing smarter inbound intercept — reaching out to the high-intent people who already showed up, before those people talk to a competitor.

Your website is telling you who's ready.

Most CRMs are too slow to listen.

## Context

2026 B2B intent data research confirms that behavioral signals — repeat site visits, pricing page engagement, email open patterns — are stronger predictors of deal close probability than any manually entered pipeline stage. The gap: most CRMs only reflect deals where a prospect explicitly raised their hand (form fill, demo request, inbound call). The 80%+ of intent that shows up in website behavior and email analytics is systematically invisible. Free-game pillar delivers a practical reframe on how to read real pipeline.
