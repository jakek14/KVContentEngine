## Post

Google AI Overviews now appear on 48% of search results.

When they do, organic click-through rates drop 61%.

58.5% of all searches end without a single click.

Your SEO traffic isn't declining because your content got worse. The search surface changed.

First-order effect: less traffic from organic. Every founder should update their assumptions now, not next year.

Second-order effect — this is the useful part: the visitors who DO click are further into their journey than they used to be. They already read the AI summary. They're on your site because the algorithm couldn't answer what they actually needed.

That's a different visitor. Higher intent. Less discovery, more evaluation.

The right strategic question for the next three years isn't "how do I rank?"

It's: what distribution channels don't have a Google-shaped dependency?

Email lists. Direct traffic. Repeat visitors. Content that drives DMs, not just clicks. Communities where you're a known voice.

The search channel is contracting structurally. The founders building owned audiences right now are making a bet that will look obvious in 24 months.

Start building what you'd have if Google didn't exist.
