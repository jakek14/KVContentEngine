## Post

The creative is the targeting.

Three years ago you won on Meta by building the right audience. Demographics. Interests. Lookalikes. Retargeting pools.

That's not the primary lever anymore.

Meta's algorithm reads your ad to decide who sees it. The hook, the visual, the specific language — those signals tell the algorithm which users to find.

Which means the old playbook is backwards.

Instead of: build audiences → run ads
It's now: build creative so specific that the algorithm finds the audience for you.

For a small marketing team, this is actually good news.

Big brands have more budget for audience data. They don't necessarily have better creative instincts.

If you understand your customer's specific problem — the words they use, the fears they have, the outcome they want — you can out-create companies that out-spend you.

You don't need a massive media budget to win on creative.

You need a deep understanding of who you're talking to.
