## Post

67% of B2B marketing teams still rely on last-touch attribution.

And I get why — it's measurable. You can show the final click. Report it in the dashboard. Call it a win.

But last-touch tells you where someone ended up. Not why they chose you.

The real work happens before the trackable moment. Podcast someone listened to. Peer recommendation in a Slack group. LinkedIn post they read three weeks ago. None of that shows up in your analytics.

Optimizing purely for last-touch is like judging a book by its last page.

The shift I'm trying to make as a marketer: stop asking "what click drove this conversion" and start asking "what made this person trust us enough to convert at all."

That's a harder question. But it's the right one.
