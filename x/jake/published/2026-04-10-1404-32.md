## Post

The old outbound playbook: build a big list, send volume, hope for 1-2%.

New one: identify who's already showing intent and reach them first.

Over 70% of B2B companies now use intent data for targeting. The ones winning at outbound aren't sending more emails — they're sending better ones to people who already have a reason to respond.

Think about it from the buyer's side. They researched your category, visited pricing pages, consumed content about the problem your product solves. Then nothing.

The brand that reaches them in that window with something relevant wins. The one that shows up six weeks later with "just checking in" doesn't.

Timing and relevance are the actual levers. Volume is a distraction.

The top marketers I look up to have all figured this out. The question is whether the rest of us catch up before our competitors do.
