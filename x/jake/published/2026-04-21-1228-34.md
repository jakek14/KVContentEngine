## Post

The hardest part of marketing KnownVisitors isn't writing the copy.

It's that most of our ICP hasn't realized they have the problem yet.

People running paid ads think about clicks, CPCs, conversion rates, landing page tests.

They're not thinking about the visitor who landed on their site, read two pages, and left without filling out a form.

That person vanished. No trace. No data. No way to follow up.

Most marketers have accepted that as just... how websites work.

So a lot of what we put out isn't about KnownVisitors at all.

It's about making anonymous traffic feel expensive.

Making bounce rate feel like lost pipeline, not just a metric to monitor.

Making people feel the gap between "we got traffic" and "we know who was here."

Because until someone feels that gap, they're not looking for a solution.

This is the real work in early-stage B2B marketing: you're not competing with alternatives, you're competing with "I didn't know this was a problem."

That's a harder fight. And a more interesting one.
