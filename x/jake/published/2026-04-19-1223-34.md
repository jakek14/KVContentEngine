## Post

84% of CMOs say ROI is their #1 metric for budget allocation right now.

That's leading to a predictable cut: brand budgets are getting gutted to feed performance channels.

The logic makes sense in a spreadsheet. Performance has trackable returns. Brand doesn't.

The problem: brand is what makes performance work.

You can't indefinitely retarget people who've never heard of you. At some point you need an audience that already knows what you do.

On a small bootstrapped team, you never had a separate brand budget to begin with. You get one dollar and it has to build the brand AND drive action at the same time.

That constraint forces better marketing.

When every piece of content has to do both — when you can't afford to just "build awareness" with nothing attached — you end up making things that are actually useful.

And useful things build brand faster than awareness campaigns anyway.
