## Post

The best thing about marketing at a bootstrapped company:

No budget for vanity.

Every campaign either moves pipeline or it gets cut. No "brand awareness" work nobody can trace. No conference logos. No ad spend that "builds equity" with zero attribution.

At companies with real budgets, marketers can hide inside activity that feels productive but isn't.

At a bootstrapped company, the metrics are honest.

Turns out that's one of the best conditions for actually learning marketing. You find out fast what matters.
