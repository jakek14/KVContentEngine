## Post

This is written about software. But it describes marketing with AI exactly.

The bottleneck isn't the tools.

It's knowing whether the output is actually good.

Anyone can generate 10 ad copy variations in 90 seconds. Anyone can produce a full campaign brief with one prompt. The tools are genuinely impressive and getting better fast.

But if you can't tell which version is actually good — not just grammatically correct, but compelling, differentiated, right for the audience — you're not doing faster marketing.

You're generating more average work, faster.

The marketers getting real leverage from AI aren't the ones using it the most. They're the ones who've built enough judgment to evaluate what comes out.

AI raises the floor. Taste still sets the ceiling.
