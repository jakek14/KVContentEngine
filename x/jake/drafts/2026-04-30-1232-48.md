## Post

Google AI Max moved out of beta this month.

The migration from standard search campaigns is happening. More automation. Less manual control over keywords, bidding, audience targeting.

The push-back I keep seeing: losing control of the execution layer.

That's the wrong worry.

The deeper risk is what happens when you stop thinking about strategy because it feels like AI should handle that too.

You hand over the keywords, then the bidding, then the audience definition — and eventually the brief becomes "just optimize for conversions." No real ICP. No message rationale. No clarity on why someone should choose you over the next result.

AI executes well. It doesn't decide what matters.

The marketers keeping their edge aren't protecting manual controls. They're staying upstream — owning the brief, the audience insight, the message architecture.

That layer never gets automated.
