## Post

73% of DTC brands responded to rising tariff costs by cutting their marketing budgets.

The brands that raised prices and leaned harder into value messaging saw 15% higher conversion rates — even with a 20% price increase.

This pattern keeps showing up.

When costs go up, the instinct is always to spend less on marketing. Go quiet. Wait it out. The thinking is some version of "we can't afford to advertise right now."

But going quiet doesn't reduce your costs. It just reduces the number of people who know you exist.

When tariffs hit your whole category, everyone's costs go up. Prices rise across the board. The brands that stay visible and clearly explain why they're worth buying — at the new price — win market share from the ones who disappeared.

The brands that cut spend and hoped tariffs would reverse? A lot of them are going to have a very hard time getting back in front of buyers who forgot about them.

Under pressure is exactly when marketing clarity matters most.
