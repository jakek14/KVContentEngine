## Post

Cursor 3 just dropped a multi-agent interface.

You describe what you want. Multiple agents write it, test it, ship it.

Their framing: you're not the coder anymore — you're the manager.

Here's what I actually think about that:

The harder you lean on AI to build, the more it matters that you know what "good" looks like.

The tool got faster. The judgment still has to come from you.

## Context

Cursor 3 launched in early April with a major platform shift: a multi-agent interface where developers describe what they want to build in plain language, and a coordinated team of AI agents executes it in parallel. The "you're now the manager" reframe from Cursor's team is compelling — and also worth stress-testing. Jake's take is real: as AI coding tools get more powerful, the bottleneck shifts from execution to judgment.
