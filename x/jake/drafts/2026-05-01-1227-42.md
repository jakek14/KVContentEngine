## Post

**Tweet 1**
B2B buyers are shortlisting vendors on social before they've ever searched for them.

Social commerce just crossed 20% of online retail in 2026. And this isn't just a D2C story.

It's a marketing strategy story.

**Tweet 2**
The old B2B funnel: problem → Google search → website → demo.

The new reality: in-feed content → brand familiarity → shortlist → demo request.

Buyers form opinions about vendors in the scroll. Not at the search bar.

**Tweet 3**
This changes the job.

Getting found by someone with an active buying problem still matters.

But the brands winning big are already in the consideration set before the problem gets bad enough to search.

Social content is how you get there.

**Tweet 4**
What I'm writing for now:

The buyer who'll need what we're selling in 6 months — not just the one searching today.

They're on social right now. They're forming opinions. They're building a mental shortlist they don't even know is a shortlist yet.

**Tweet 5**
The metrics that matter here aren't just click-through rates.

Follower growth. Content saves. Message requests. Consistent impressions from the same accounts.

Those are early signals from people at the top of a buying journey that hasn't started yet.

**Tweet 6**
Social commerce at 20% doesn't mean everyone is buying on Instagram.

It means the consideration layer has moved.

The marketers building social presence now will have a structural advantage in every funnel that starts there later.
