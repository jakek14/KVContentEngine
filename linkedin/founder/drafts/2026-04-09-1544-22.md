## Post

Tariffs just made every website visitor worth more.

Not metaphorically. Literally.

---

When your product margins get compressed by 15-45% overnight — which is what's happening to D2C brands with Chinese or global supply chains right now — the math on paid acquisition changes completely.

You used to spend $25 to acquire a customer and make $40 back. That worked.

Now you spend $25 to acquire a customer and make $22 back. That doesn't.

---

The brands that come out of this cycle ahead aren't going to be the ones who found cheaper suppliers faster.

They're going to be the ones who stopped treating website traffic as disposable.

Every visitor you paid to get is now a more expensive asset. Whether you can identify them, re-engage them, and convert them without paying to acquire them again is the question that separates growing brands from ones caught in a cash-flow spiral.

---

This isn't a crisis for brands who already built owned audience infrastructure.

It's a crisis for brands who were always one ad budget away from going dark.

The tariff squeeze is just exposing something that was already structurally broken.

---

## Context

US tariff changes in 2026 have created a margin compression event for D2C brands. The logical implication: when margins compress, every dollar of acquisition spend becomes harder to justify, and owned audience infrastructure (email lists, visitor identification, direct relationships) becomes the most durable competitive asset.
