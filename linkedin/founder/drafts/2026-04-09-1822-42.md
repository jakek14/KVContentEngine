## Post

AI broke the math on bootstrapping vs. raising.

---

The old equation was simple: capital buys headcount, headcount buys leverage, leverage beats the smaller team.

That's why raising made sense even when your runway was fine. Scale the team, move faster, win.

---

That math is fundamentally different now.

A lean team with the right AI stack can do what used to require 3x the people. Design, development, marketing, sales outreach — AI tools have collapsed the cost of execution across every function.

---

This doesn't mean bootstrapping is always right. Some companies need distribution advantages or sales capacity that capital genuinely accelerates.

But for a growing slice of SaaS founders, bootstrapping is a more competitive option than it's ever been. Not because it's easier — it isn't. Because the leverage gap between funded and unfunded teams narrowed.

---

The founders I'm watching most closely in 2026 aren't chasing the best term sheet.

They're figuring out how lean they can operate at full output.

That's the new bootstrapping.
