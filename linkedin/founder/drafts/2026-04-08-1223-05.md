## Post

Cursor just shipped a multi-agent coding interface.

You describe what you want to build. A team of AI agents writes it, tests it, ships it.

Their framing: you're no longer the coder. You're the manager.

---

Here's what this actually means for founders building with AI:

The barrier to building isn't "can I write code." It hasn't been for a while.

The new barrier is whether you can think clearly enough about what you want to build.

Can you spec the feature with enough precision that an AI team can execute it?

Can you review the output and know when something's wrong?

Can you catch the edge case that works locally and breaks in production?

These are judgment skills — not technical skills. And they're the ones that don't show up in any "vibe coding" tutorial.

---

The founders winning with AI-assisted development right now aren't necessarily the ones with the most coding knowledge.

They're the ones who know their domain cold — and can communicate it precisely to tools that execute at 10x the speed.

The code gets written by agents now.

The clarity still has to come from you.
