## Post

Solo founders are having their best year ever.

One founder hit $62K MRR in 90 days. Another built a $28K/month portfolio with zero prior code knowledge. The micro-SaaS market is growing 30% annually and headed toward $60B by 2030.

This isn't luck. It's structural.

---

Here's what actually changed:

AI tools cut time-to-product from months to weeks. You can validate, iterate, and ship without a co-founder or a headcount. The entry cost is near zero.

But here's what most people miss: the tools are table stakes now. Everyone has them.

The edge is knowing which problem to solve — and staying small enough to solve it well.

---

The playbook I keep seeing work in 2026:

1. Pick a problem in an industry you know cold. Your distribution starts with your own relationships, not cold outreach.

2. Build an ugly MVP with AI tools. If real people pay for the ugly version, you've found the need.

3. Keep the team at zero as long as possible. Premature hiring is the fastest way to kill a good unit margin.

4. Use revenue as your scoreboard. Not users. Not traffic. Revenue.

---

The founding advantage in 2026 isn't technical. It's specific.

The founders winning are the ones who picked a narrow problem they already understood — and then used every AI tool available to build it faster than anyone thought possible.

The window to do this is open right now. It will not always be this open.
