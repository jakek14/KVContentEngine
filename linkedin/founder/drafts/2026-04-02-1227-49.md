## Post

CAC is up 40-60% since 2023. The founders quietly winning on pipeline aren't spending more on ads.

They're posting.

---

I know "post more content" sounds like advice from a LinkedIn coach selling a $997 course.

But the numbers are hard to ignore. Early-stage SaaS founders who build consistent personal brands are seeing 70-80% of their early pipeline come from organic content — not ads, not cold outreach, not conference booths.

And with paid CAC at all-time highs, the ROI math has never been more lopsided.

---

Here's what actually works — and what doesn't.

What works:

1. Frameworks, not observations. "CAC is rising" is an observation. "Here are the 3 signals I watch to decide when to cut paid and double down on organic" is a framework. Frameworks get saved. Observations get scrolled past.

2. Specific numbers over vague takeaways. "We cut churn 30% with one change to our onboarding email" outperforms "focus on onboarding" every time. Specifics signal credibility.

3. Honest losses as much as wins. The post about what didn't work gets 3-4x the engagement of the win post. Founders are exhausted by the highlight reel.

4. Short posts regularly > long posts occasionally. Consistent short takes build a daily relationship with your audience. Save long form for the ideas that actually need the space.

What doesn't work:

— Writing for everyone instead of your specific buyer
— Posting daily for a week, disappearing for two months, repeat
— "Thought leadership" that says nothing specific
— Engagement bait

---

The founders building real pipeline from content in 2026 aren't better writers.

They're just more consistent than the ones who quit after three posts go unnoticed.

The cheapest acquisition channel you have is already on your phone.
