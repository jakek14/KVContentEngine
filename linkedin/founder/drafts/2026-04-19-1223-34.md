## Post

Marketing budgets are getting squeezed right now.

Economic uncertainty. Tariff caution. Every CFO wants proof of return before approving anything.

The predictable outcome: performance channels are winning, brand is getting gutted.

---

That logic makes sense in a spreadsheet.

It's also a slow bleed.

Here's what I've watched happen when companies cut brand aggressively to feed performance:

Their performance ads start running against colder and colder audiences. CAC creeps up. Return on spend drops. They cut further to compensate, which makes it worse.

---

Brand isn't separate from performance.

It's the substrate that makes performance work.

The companies coming out of this environment strongest aren't the ones who chose one or the other.

They're the ones who invested — before the squeeze hit — in owned audience infrastructure that doesn't have a CPM.

An email list they actually built. A retargeting audience of people who already visited their site. Content that creates familiarity without a media buy behind every piece.

---

The founders who did that work when times were good are now running performance campaigns against warm audiences.

The ones who didn't are learning why brand matters the hard way.
