## Post

The pricing mistake that's hardest to see when you're in it: charging too little.

Not for the obvious revenue reasons.

For what it signals to the market.

---

In 2026, there's a growing consensus among SaaS founders that low prices have become synonymous with instability.

Not to all buyers. But to the buyers you actually want.

Serious B2B buyers aren't optimizing for the cheapest option.

They're looking for signals that the company they're betting on will still exist in 24 months.

Pricing is one of those signals.

A tool priced like it's afraid to charge isn't inspiring confidence.

---

When you underprice, you attract:

— Buyers who churn the moment a cheaper alternative surfaces

— High-volume, low-margin accounts that stretch your support thin and generate noise, not insight

— A feedback loop from users who won't pay more even when you add features — because they chose you based on price, not value

---

The founders who break through the underpricing trap usually stumble into it.

They raise prices when they're desperate for cash.

Then they discover their conversion rate didn't drop.

Sometimes it went up.

Not because the product changed. Because they finally looked like what they actually were: a serious tool for serious buyers.

---

Pricing isn't just economics.

It's positioning. It's filtering. It's telling the market who you're for.

Charge like a serious company. Attract serious customers.

The ones who stay, expand, and refer you — they're not the ones you acquired at the lowest price point.

They're the ones who made a deliberate choice to invest.

That difference compounds every month.
