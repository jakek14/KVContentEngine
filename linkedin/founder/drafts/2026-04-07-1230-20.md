## Post

Software is trading at a discount to the S&P 500 for the first time in history.

Let that land.

The iShares software ETF is down 21%+ this year and roughly 30% from its September 2025 peak. SaaStr called it "The SaaS Rout of 2026." For the first time ever, the market is pricing software below the broader market — not at a premium.

Median monthly revenue churn has ticked up from 11.34% to 12.50%. Series A now requires $1-2M ARR, NRR above 110%, LTV:CAC 3:1+, and CAC payback under 12 months. The bar didn't just move. It got a lot higher.

---

What this moment is actually telling founders:

The market is done rewarding growth-at-any-cost metrics. Investors and buyers both want proof of real, durable demand. Not vanity traffic numbers. Not inflated MQL counts. Actual signals that the right buyers care — and keep caring after they sign up.

Here's the thing: the best early demand signal isn't in your ad account. It's on your website.

Which companies are visiting? What pages are they hitting? How often are they coming back? A prospect who's been to your pricing page three times in two weeks is telling you something your outbound list never could.

That intent data exists right now. Most founders have no idea they're sitting on it.

---

I built KnownVisitors because I kept watching early-stage founders spend $10-50k/month on ads and have zero visibility into who actually showed up. They'd optimize CTR, A/B test headlines, and argue about which ad creative performed better — while dozens of qualified buyers were visiting, browsing, and leaving without a trace.

Anonymous traffic isn't just a lead gen problem. It's a demand signal you're throwing away.

---

The SaaS rout is a reset, not a collapse. The companies that come out of it with real pipeline, real retention, and clean NRR metrics will be fine.

The ones optimizing vanity metrics in a down market won't.

Know who's already interested. Everything else gets cheaper from there.
