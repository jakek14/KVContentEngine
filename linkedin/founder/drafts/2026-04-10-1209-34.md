## Post

Down markets don't break companies.

They reveal which parts of your funnel were always broken.

---

In an up market, some percentage of everything converts. Enough deals close that you never find out which leads were real signals and which were just noise. The funnel looks fine because you're never forced to optimize it.

Then the market tightens. Volume drops. And you learn fast.

---

The companies I've watched survive and grow through tough cycles didn't build better funnels when things got hard.

They already had them.

Specifically, they had visibility into which signals actually mattered.

Which accounts kept coming back to the site.

Which leads engaged with content more than once.

Which buyers hit the pricing page without ever booking a demo.

Most companies don't know any of this. Their CRM shows pipeline that people opted into. Their analytics shows sessions without identities. They have a lot of data and very little signal.

---

The founders who build through down markets stop chasing cold leads and start acting on warm intent.

Here's what that looks like practically:

Behavioral triggers over drip sequences. If someone revisits your site three times in a week, they get a personal note — not email #4 from a six-week nurture sequence.

Pricing page attention is a closing signal. Not a data point. An action item.

First-party behavior beats third-party lists. Every time. The people already on your site are warmer than anyone you're cold-reaching.

---

That signal layer is what most SaaS companies are operating without.

It's also exactly what we built KnownVisitors to fix — converting anonymous session data into named, contactable, intent-qualified leads before they disappear.

The down market isn't your problem.

Running a funnel blind is the problem.
