## Post

A 2-person team can now outship a 20-person engineering org.

Bloomberg called it a "productivity panic" inside big tech. Engineers watching AI agents do in minutes what used to take days. Managers quietly questioning headcount. VPs of Engineering suddenly having to justify the size of their teams.

The panic makes sense if you're a big company.

For founders, it feels like the opposite.

Six months ago, my workflow looked like this: spec a feature, hand it off, wait a week, review, iterate, deploy. Two or three weeks from idea to shipped — minimum.

Now: describe what I want in plain English, watch it get built in real time, push to production the same day.

Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot — pick your tool. The point is, the cost of coding has never been lower. AI tools are delivering 25-55% productivity gains per developer. For a small founding team, it's not a 25% lift. It's a qualitative shift — the kind that used to require raising $3M to hire a full engineering team.

The bottleneck isn't code anymore.

It's ideas. Distribution. Customer insight. Hard decisions.

All the things that always separated great founders from the rest — AI didn't touch any of that.

It just cleared the table of one more excuse for not building.

If you've been sitting on an idea because "I can't code" or "I don't have the budget to hire engineers" — that excuse expired.

What are you waiting for?
