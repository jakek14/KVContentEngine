## Post

Conversion chance drops 80% if you wait longer than 5 minutes to follow up.

80%.

Most sales teams respond in hours. Some in days.

---

Here's what that gap actually costs you:

The person who filled out your form at 2:17 PM on Tuesday was in a buying window. They were comparing options, doing research, ready to talk. By the time your rep sees it at 9 AM Wednesday, that window has mostly closed.

Speed isn't a tactic. It's a strategy.

---

The best-performing outbound sequences I've seen have one thing in common: they trigger on behavior, not on a schedule.

Someone visits your pricing page twice in a week? That's intent. Follow up that day — not next week on your drip sequence.

Someone watches your demo video to the end? That's a signal. Your rep should know within the hour.

The data exists. Most companies just don't act on it fast enough.

---

The brands closing the most deals in a down market aren't the ones with the biggest lists.

They're the ones with the shortest gap between "this person showed interest" and "we reached out."

What does your current lead response time look like?
