## Post

The companies winning in 2026 aren't winning on product. They're winning on distribution.

---

Here's the shift happening underneath a lot of the noise right now:

Building is cheap.

With AI coding tools, a reasonably technical founder can ship a working SaaS in days. The barrier to entry has collapsed. Every niche is getting more crowded, faster.

But distribution didn't get cheaper.

Reaching the right buyer at the right moment — before they've already chosen a competitor — is still hard. In some ways it's harder than it's ever been. AI search means fewer organic clicks. Ad costs keep climbing. Attention is more fractured across more surfaces.

The result: building is commoditized. Distribution is the moat.

---

What does a distribution moat look like in 2026?

It looks like owning your audience — not renting it from Google or Meta.

It looks like knowing that someone visited your pricing page twice this week, found you through a search for your product category, and opened your last email but didn't click. That's a buyer you can reach before they finish comparing you with a competitor.

Most companies have no visibility into those signals. They're spending on acquisition while staying completely blind to what's happening to the traffic they already paid for.

---

That's the insight behind KnownVisitors.

Distribution is hard enough when you can see what's working. It's nearly impossible when most of your traffic is invisible.

The product moat was always temporary. Every feature gets copied.

The distribution moat is what compounds — because owning the relationship means you can keep improving, keep reaching, keep converting.

Build the distribution infrastructure first. Everything else catches up.
