## Post

Q1 2026 broke every startup funding record.

$297 billion raised in a single quarter.

Here's what the headline doesn't say:

Nearly all of it went to six companies.

OpenAI: $122B. Anthropic: $30B. xAI: $20B. Waymo: $16B.

Four deals. Roughly $190 billion.

The river isn't bigger. It just got rerouted.

---

For non-AI SaaS founders trying to raise right now, this is the context missing from every "funding is back" narrative.

Total capital deployed is at an all-time high. Median round sizes are not.

The money exists. The appetite for everything that isn't AI infrastructure is more competitive than it's been in years.

---

What changes if you accept this:

Either build in the AI infrastructure layer — where the capital is genuinely flowing — or build a business that doesn't require VC capital to reach your goals.

The middle path is harder than it's been in a decade.

Bootstrapped SaaS and AI-native companies are both doing fine. The category in between — traditional SaaS looking to raise a Series A in a non-AI vertical — is facing a legitimately rough environment.

The funding headlines are real. The math underneath them is the part worth understanding.
