## Post

Longer free trials don't convert better. The data says the opposite.

7-day free trials convert at 40.4%.

Trials over 60 days? 30.6%.

---

Most founders extend their free trial thinking: more time equals more value equals more conversions.

The logic is backward.

A free trial is not a patience test.

It's a race to the aha moment.

---

When the trial is too long, three things happen:

First, urgency evaporates. Users sign up fully intending to explore later — and later never comes.

Second, your onboarding sequence becomes background noise. By day 14, they've already decided whether to care. Your follow-up emails feel like spam.

Third, the customer who was going to convert has compared alternatives, talked to your competitors, and moved on while you were waiting for them to get settled in.

---

The constraint forces the activation.

Short trial with a fast aha moment creates a conversion signal.

Long trial with slow onboarding creates abandoned accounts and data you can't trust.

---

The question I'd push founders to ask: it's not "how much time does the user need?"

It's "how fast can we get them to feel the value?"

That's an onboarding problem, not a trial-length problem.

The founders solving for the latter are optimizing the wrong variable.

---

What's your current trial length — and at what point in the journey do users actually "get it"?

That gap is your real optimization opportunity.
