## Post

94% of U.S. advertisers are worried about tariff impact on their ad budgets.

45% are already cutting spend.

If you're running a business that depends on paid acquisition right now, this isn't a headline. It's a decision you need to make.

---

Here's what I keep coming back to: when your paid budget gets compressed, every visitor you already paid to acquire becomes exponentially more valuable.

The math is brutal in one direction and clarifying in another.

When CPMs were low and margins were healthy, waste was acceptable. You could let 98% of your visitors bounce anonymously, run more ads, get more traffic. Net-net it worked.

That's not the environment right now.

Tariffs are squeezing margins on sourced inventory. Ad costs that were already up 40-60% since 2023 are now getting cut entirely. The founders and marketers who kept paid acquisition on autopilot are waking up to budgets they can no longer justify.

---

The shift I'm seeing from the people paying attention:

The question is moving from "how do we get more traffic?" to "how do we get more out of the traffic we have?"

That's the right question.

If you're spending $15 CPM and 98% of your visitors leave without identifying themselves — that's not a traffic problem. It's a data problem. A "we paid for this person and lost them forever" problem.

That's exactly why I built KnownVisitors: identify every visitor who lands on your site — name, email, phone, company — no forms, no friction. So when ad budgets get cut and every click costs more, you're building a list, not just buying traffic.

---

The brands that come out of this moment stronger won't be the ones that spent the most.

They'll be the ones that wasted the least.
