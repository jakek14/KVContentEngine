## Post

Horizontal SaaS is down 35%. Vertical AI is growing 400%.

The numbers aren't subtle.

For years, "platform thinking" was the dominant startup strategy. Build something broad. Expand into adjacent verticals. Become the operating system for your customer's workflow.

That bet is collapsing.

When AI can natively coordinate across tools, write code, analyze data, and manage workflows — what is the value of a platform that does all of those things averagely? The horizontal tool's advantage was consolidation. AI gives you consolidation without the lock-in.

What's actually surviving the AI era:

— Products with proprietary data that AI can't replicate from scratch
— Tools built around specific compliance or regulatory requirements
— Categories where deep domain knowledge is the actual product
— Companies that understand their customer's problem at a level no generalist tool ever will

When I was deciding what to build with KnownVisitors, I wasn't consciously running this framework. But looking back, we made a bet on specificity over breadth.

Visitor identification isn't a horizontal play. It requires knowing what makes a valid lead record, how to handle compliance across multiple jurisdictions, how to clean and verify data before it ever touches a sales team. You can't layer a general-purpose AI on top of that and call it done.

Generic software is getting compressed from above.

The founders who come out of the next 24 months in the strongest position will be the ones who went deep on a specific problem — not wide.

Specificity is the moat.
