## Post

Only 3% of your target market is actively shopping right now.

The other 97% are not buyers — yet.

---

Most B2B marketing is built for demand capture: PPC, retargeting, bottom-of-funnel content. All of it designed for buyers who are already in the market.

The problem: at any given moment, that's 3% of your total addressable market.

The other 97% are in research mode. Casually aware. Won't buy for another 6-18 months.

---

Here's what that means in practice:

If your entire strategy is built around capturing demand, you're competing for the same 3% as every other company in your category. It's expensive. It's crowded. It's why CAC keeps climbing.

The companies building real pipeline in 2026 are doing two things:

**1. Creating demand.** Putting content in front of the 97% who aren't shopping yet — so when they enter that 3% window, you're already top of mind. When they're ready to buy, you're the default.

**2. Identifying who's in-market.** Instead of blasting everyone and waiting for a form fill, they're identifying which visitors are actively researching, which accounts are showing intent signals, and which conversations are worth starting now.

---

Most companies do one or the other.

The ones growing efficiently do both.

The goal isn't to reach more people.

It's to be there first when the right people are ready.

## Context

The "3% rule" (often attributed to Chet Holmes' work on the buying pyramid) states that at any given moment only 3% of any target market is in active buying mode. Most B2B marketing optimization ignores this and focuses purely on demand capture. The post reframes the strategy as a dual approach: creating demand for the 97% + capturing it for the 3% who are ready now.
