## Post

Most website visitors don't fill out a form.

That doesn't mean they're not buyers.

They're evaluating you right now — reading case studies, mapping your pricing, checking your docs. They just haven't raised their hand yet.

Here's what separates real buying intent from casual browsing:

---

**1. Extended time on your pricing page.**

Browsers leave in under 30 seconds. Buyers spend 3–5 minutes — sometimes more. They're doing math, not just looking.

**2. Multiple sessions in a short window.**

One visit is curiosity. Three visits across a single week is evaluation. Someone is building an internal business case.

**3. Docs and pricing in the same session.**

This is the most reliable signal. When someone maps implementation complexity against cost in one sitting, that's procurement thinking — not browsing.

**4. Traffic from branded search.**

Organic branded visits mean they already know you exist. They're not discovering — they're researching before a decision.

**5. Return visits after paid ads.**

Someone who clicked your ad and then came back organically didn't forget about you. They followed up on their own. That's a warm buyer.

---

These signals exist in your data right now.

The problem is that most companies have no way to connect behavioral signals to a real person — a name, a company, a contact they can actually act on.

That gap is where pipeline goes to die.
