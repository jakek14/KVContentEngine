## Post

The average SaaS landing page converts at 3.8%. Top performers hit 11.6%. The gap isn't the design.

Most CRO roadmaps go straight to the page: hero copy, button color, form length, social proof placement.

Those things move the needle at the margins.

The real conversion gap comes from three layers most teams don't address first.

---

**1. Message-market fit, not message clarity.**

A well-written page speaking to the wrong problem converts at the same rate as a bad page.

The question isn't "is our copy clear?" It's "are we speaking to the problem the visitor is actually trying to solve?"

Copy written at a 5th–7th grade reading level converts at 12.9%.

Professional-level copy: 2.1%.

Simpler isn't dumber. It's less friction between the idea and the decision.

---

**2. Traffic quality before traffic volume.**

Paid search traffic converts at 3.2%. Social traffic at 1.5%. Organic branded search: significantly higher.

If your conversion rate looks low, segment by source before optimizing anything.

A 3.8% rate with 80% paid social traffic is a targeting problem.

Not a CRO problem.

---

**3. Timing of the ask.**

Most landing pages ask for commitment before they've earned it.

They gate the value, present the form, and hope two lines of social proof did the heavy lifting.

Interactive demos convert 2x better than static screenshots.

Letting visitors map their own use case before the CTA consistently outperforms "one size fits all" — because it makes the form feel like a natural next step, not a toll booth.

---

We see this pattern constantly in B2B: companies spend months on button tests and hero redesigns without fixing what's actually wrong.

The upstream issues are almost always the same: speaking to the wrong problem, attracting the wrong traffic, and asking for commitment before trust is earned.

Fix those three layers.

Then A/B test the button.
