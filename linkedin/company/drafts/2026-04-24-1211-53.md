## Post

The DTC market hit $212.9 billion in 2026.

The average brand loses $29 on every new customer it acquires.

That's not a performance problem. That's a model problem.

---

The acquisition-first approach worked when CAC was lower and LTV was predictable.

You could spend to acquire, count on repeat purchases, and come out ahead.

Customer acquisition costs have increased 222% over eight years.

The math doesn't hold anymore.

---

The brands growing efficiently in 2026 aren't the ones outspending their competitors on acquisition.

They've shifted the equation:

More revenue from returning customers vs. net-new ones.

Smarter targeting — reaching fewer, better-fit buyers — instead of chasing volume.

Owned channels that don't reprice on you every quarter.

Post-purchase experiences that turn a first-time buyer into a repeat customer before they see a competitor's ad.

---

The acquisition model isn't broken because ads got worse.

It's broken because too many brands built a business that only works if the first click pays for itself.

That was always fragile.

The answer isn't better ads.

It's a fundamentally different way of thinking about growth — one that starts with the customers you already have, not the ones you haven't met yet.
