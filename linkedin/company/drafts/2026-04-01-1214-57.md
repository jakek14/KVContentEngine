## Post

AI agents just started shopping for your customers.

Conversions from AI referrals jumped 1,247% in the second half of 2025. Not a rounding error. In six months, AI-directed shopping went from a novelty to a measurable traffic source — and most brands have no idea what to do with it.

Here's what's actually happening.

Tools like Perplexity, ChatGPT, and emerging shopping agents are now actively researching products, comparing options, and in some cases completing purchases on behalf of users. The "visitor" on your site may not be a person. It's an AI browsing for one.

This changes the visit profile completely. Sessions are shorter. Behavior is non-human. The standard engagement signals you've been optimizing for — time on page, scroll depth, click patterns — don't apply the same way.

What this means for your brand right now:

1. Your attribution is probably off.
AI referrals often appear as direct traffic in standard analytics. Unexplained direct spikes? You might already be in the mix — and not know it.

2. Your product data needs to be machine-readable.
AI agents pull from structured data — schema markup, product feeds, rich descriptions. Thin metadata means you won't be recommended, full stop.

3. The research-to-purchase gap just got wider.
An AI browses. A human buys. That gap — between AI-led discovery and human conversion — is where brands are leaking revenue right now.

The brands getting ahead of this aren't panicking. They're auditing their feeds, tightening their schema markup, and actually looking at where their traffic comes from.

Agentic commerce isn't on the horizon. It already arrived.
