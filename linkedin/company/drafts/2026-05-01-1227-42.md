## Post

The AI content flood is real. And most of it is mediocre.

---

Every week, B2B marketing teams are publishing AI-generated posts, articles, and landing pages that are grammatically clean, structurally sound, and say absolutely nothing specific.

Correct. Just empty.

The irony: the tool designed to make content creation easier has made it harder to get attention. Because when the supply of content explodes, the threshold for getting read goes up with it.

---

Here's our take: companies with genuinely expert, opinionated, specific content have more signal advantage right now than at any point in the last decade.

When every competitor's content sounds the same — same structure, same hooks, same three-bullet takeaway — the company that says something true and specific cuts through like a signal in noise.

B2B buyers have been reading AI-generated content for two years now. They've developed pattern recognition for it. They can feel the absence of a real perspective before they finish the first paragraph.

What they can't ignore: something that makes them reconsider a belief they held before clicking.

---

Write the thing only your company could write.

Not the thing any company could have generated in 30 seconds.

That's the moat that doesn't get replicated.
