## Post

By Q2 2026, 60% of Chrome users will have opted out of cookie tracking.

Not blocked by Google — opted out by choice.

---

Add Safari's third-party cookie block (in place since 2020) and Firefox's default tracker filtering, and the picture becomes clear: the website analytics most brands rely on are working with a significantly degraded dataset.

Your GA4 dashboard still shows sessions, traffic sources, pageviews.

It looks normal. It isn't.

The data behind those numbers represents a shrinking share of your actual audience. Decisions that used to be informed by near-complete visibility are now being made on partial information — and most teams don't even know it.

---

The brands adapting to this aren't just updating their consent banners.

They're changing the fundamental question:

From "how many people visited?" to "who visited?"

Count-based analytics tells you scale.

Identity-based intelligence tells you who to reach, when, and with what.

That's a very different data problem — and a very different solution.
