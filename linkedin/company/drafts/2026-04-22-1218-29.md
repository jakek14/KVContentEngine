## Post

Two-thirds of knowledge workers are still remote.

And your B2B visitor tracking probably doesn't know they exist.

---

Traditional website visitor identification works by matching IP addresses to companies.

It was built for an office-first world.

When a director of procurement visits your site from a corporate office, their IP resolves to their employer. The system works.

When that same person works from home, their IP resolves to Comcast or Verizon.

Your identification tool sees a consumer. Skips it.

That prospect just became invisible.

---

In 2026, this isn't an edge case.

It's the majority of your B2B traffic.

Your most valuable visitors — senior buyers, decision-makers, high-intent researchers — have the same authority and intent they always did.

They're just working from the wrong address.

---

The companies solving this have moved beyond IP matching.

They're layering identity graphs, first-party signals, behavioral data, and verified contact matching to build an accurate picture — regardless of where someone logs in from.

That's the standard we hold at KnownVisitors for every record we deliver.

Not just company identification. Name, email, phone — verified, compliant, ready to use.

Because in a remote-first world, the IP address stopped being the identity a long time ago.

---

The hybrid work era changed where people work.

It also changed what B2B visitor data actually looks like.

Most identification tools haven't caught up.
