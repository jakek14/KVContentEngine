## Post

It now costs $2 to acquire $1 of new ARR.

That's the median in 2026. Up 14% from 2023. Bottom quartile companies are at $2.82 per dollar of ARR.

---

This number matters more than most founders realize.

If you're spending $2 to get $1 of revenue, payback period has become the most important metric in your business — not growth rate, not logo count, not MRR headline.

---

Here's what the teams managing this well are doing differently:

They're not trying to cut CAC through cheaper ads. That usually cuts quality, attracts worse fits, and extends payback further.

They're cutting CAC through better qualification — making sure every acquisition dollar goes toward buyers who are likely to close fast and stay long.

The best acquisition isn't always the fastest or cheapest. It's the one that recovers cost inside 12 months and retains past month 24.

---

The efficiency era doesn't mean growth is dead.

It means the math has to work at every step of the funnel — from first click to renewal.

Know your CAC payback. Build the entire funnel backward from there.
