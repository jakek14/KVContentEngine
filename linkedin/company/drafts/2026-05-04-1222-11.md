## Post

1 in 6 marketing emails never reaches the inbox.

That's not a worst-case scenario. That's the global average.

Here's what changed in 2026 that most marketing teams haven't fully absorbed:

**Open rates are no longer a reliable metric.**
Apple Mail pre-fetches all email images through its own proxy — regardless of whether the recipient actually opens. Apple Mail holds roughly 50% of email client market share. Your reported open rates are inflated. Optimizing subject lines based on this data is optimizing for noise.

**Authentication is the floor, not a feature.**
Senders without proper SPF, DKIM, and DMARC authentication see inbox placement drop to 44%. Gmail's inbox rate has fallen from 89.8% to 84.2% over the last 18 months. Microsoft sits at 75.6%. If your email stack isn't fully authenticated, you're starting from a disadvantage before a single send.

**The mistake most teams are making:**
Using AI to generate more emails faster. Volume is not a strategy — it's how you damage your sender reputation and bury your engaged subscribers at the same time.

The email programs winning right now are sending fewer, better-targeted emails to engaged lists. Deliverability is doing the distribution work.

Every subject line test, nurture sequence, and promotional campaign is built on a foundation you can't see. If that foundation is broken, none of the surface-level work matters.

Deliverability comes first.
