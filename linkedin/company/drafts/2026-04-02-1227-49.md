## Post

Meta just removed credit card billing for high-spend accounts — effective this week.

If your account spends ~$50K+/month and you're connected to a Business Portfolio, you're now on monthly invoicing or direct debit. No opt-out.

That's not the headline.

---

The headline is the location fees.

Meta added a 2-5% surcharge on all ad spend in the UK, France, Italy, Austria, Spain, and Turkey — to cover Digital Services Taxes. Effective April 2026.

If you're running international campaigns, your CPMs just got more expensive overnight. No targeting changes needed. No notice required.

---

Paid reach was already shrinking. Now it's also getting a quiet surcharge in 6 major markets.

The brands that weather this best aren't necessarily the ones with the biggest budgets.

They're the ones extracting the most value from every visitor that already lands on their site.

## Context

Meta made two significant billing changes effective early April 2026: (1) removed credit card payments for accounts spending ~$50K+/month connected to a Business Portfolio — now forced to monthly invoicing or direct debit; (2) added 2-5% location fees for UK, France, Italy, Austria, Spain, and Turkey to cover Digital Services Taxes. Billing settings were locked March 30–April 4 during the transition. This is a timely, practical hot-take for brands running Meta ads at scale.
