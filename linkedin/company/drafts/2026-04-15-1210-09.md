## Post

Per-seat pricing dropped from 21% to 15% of SaaS companies in just 12 months.

Gartner says 70% of businesses will prefer usage-based models over per-seat by 2026.

The model that built most of the SaaS industry is quietly becoming the exception, not the default.

Here's why:

AI changed what a "seat" actually means.

When an AI agent can handle tasks that previously required a named employee with access to your software, pricing per seat starts to feel like pricing per typewriter key.

The value isn't in how many people log in. It's in what the software actually produces — leads generated, tickets resolved, workflows executed, contacts identified.

The SaaS companies adapting well aren't just switching pricing models. They're rethinking the fundamental question: what's the one unit of value our customers actually care about?

That's the value metric. And it should drive everything — packaging, pricing, expansion, retention.

The companies that get this right are seeing 31% higher customer retention and 21% higher satisfaction scores.

The ones still optimizing for seat count are optimizing for a model that's fading.

What's your SaaS pricing model built on today — seats, usage, or outcomes?
