## Post

TripleDart just dropped their 2026 State of SaaS PPC Benchmark Report.

$60M+ in managed ad spend analyzed. 84 actively managed accounts. This is real data, not surveys.

Here are the findings worth knowing:

---

**UGC-style ads outperform polished brand creative by 41% in CTR and 19% in conversion rate.**

Most B2B companies default to polished creative: designed ads, branded colors, hero dashboard screenshots.

Clean. Professional. Immediately identifiable as an ad.

UGC-style means raw, native, human — screen recordings, talking-head videos, text-heavy mobile screenshots. Things that look like content, not campaigns.

It works because it doesn't trigger the "skip this" reflex. The visual language of organic content carries more trust than the visual language of advertising.

---

**Competitor conquesting campaigns deliver MQLs at 39% lower cost — despite costing 3.5x more per click.**

Average CPC for generic campaigns: $6.27. For competitor conquesting: $22.21.

The math still works because the intent is higher. Someone actively comparing you to a competitor is already in evaluation mode. You're closing a shorter gap.

---

**Platform CPL benchmarks:**
- Google Ads: $127 average CPL / 4.2% conversion rate
- LinkedIn: $213 average CPL / 2.8% conversion rate
- Meta: $94 average CPL / 3.1% conversion rate

LinkedIn's premium reflects audience quality, not platform efficiency. Meta's low CPL reflects volume, not necessarily intent.

---

Most B2B teams are allocating budget based on intuition and convention, not data like this.

The companies competing well right now aren't spending more.

They're measuring more.
