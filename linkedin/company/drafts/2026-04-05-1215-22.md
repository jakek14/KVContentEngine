## Post

Liberation Day tariffs raised average D2C product costs by 28% — in 72 hours.

China tariffs are now at 145%. The de minimis loophole — the $800 duty-free threshold that most brands with Chinese supply chains depended on — is gone. Shein and Temu have already announced price increases. D2C brands are staring down 15–45% cost increases depending on supply chain exposure.

The margin environment just changed overnight.

---

When product costs compress this fast, every dollar of marketing spend has to work harder.

Here's the math most brands aren't doing right now:

You're spending $15–25 CPM to drive visitors to your site. With tariff-driven cost increases eating into order margins, you have less room to absorb wasted ad spend. The visitors who land on your site and leave without buying aren't just missed conversions.

They're paid-for leads you have no way to follow up with.

---

The brands that will navigate this best aren't only the ones with the most resilient supply chains.

They're the ones extracting maximum value from the traffic they've already paid to acquire.

That means knowing who visited. Not just that someone did.

Website visitor identification — knowing the name, email, company, and contact info of people who browsed without filling out a form — is exactly the kind of leverage that pays for itself faster in a margin squeeze.

That's what we built KnownVisitors to do. When margins are thin, anonymous traffic is a luxury you can't afford.

---

Rising product costs will force every brand to get more disciplined about their marketing stack.

The ones who come out ahead will be the ones who stopped treating anonymous traffic as a sunk cost — and started treating it as an unfollowed lead.
