## Post

AI-native SaaS tools under $50/month average 23% gross revenue retention.

Let that land.

77% of customers who try these tools are gone within a year.

---

There's a name for this now: the "AI tourist" problem.

People spin up a tool, experiment for a week or two, generate some output, then move on before it becomes part of their workflow. The tool was easy to start. It was just as easy to leave.

---

What separates the SaaS tools that retain customers from the ones that don't isn't usually the product quality.

It's whether the value compounds over time — and specifically whether that compounding belongs to the customer or the platform.

When your insights, your data, and your workflow outputs live inside a tool you don't control, you're one cancellation away from losing all of it. Most users feel that tension even if they can't articulate it. They hold back from going all in. They keep their real data in spreadsheets "just in case."

The tools breaking the churn cycle are the ones where the data is yours. Cancel tomorrow, take everything with you. The value compounds in your business — not the vendor's system.

---

That's one of the core principles we built KnownVisitors on.

You identify your website visitors. You own that list. You take it wherever you want. No forced workflows, no data held hostage to keep you paying.

In a market where AI tourist churn is structurally baked into most tools, data ownership isn't just a differentiator.

It's what makes retention possible at all.
