## Post

CCPA 2026 changed the rules for every website using cookies or pixels.

Here's what went into effect in January:

New restrictions on how cookies and third-party pixels can collect data without explicit consent.

Data protection impact assessments are now required for any processing that presents significant risk to consumer privacy.

Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals — the universal opt-out — are now legally enforceable in California, Colorado, Connecticut, and Oregon. If a visitor has GPC enabled, your site must honor it automatically.

Fines: $7,500 per intentional violation. $2,500 per unintentional.

Most marketing teams can't tell you which third-party pixels are active on their site right now, who controls them, or whether they're triggering in ways that now require explicit consent.

---

The audit that should have happened in Q4 2025 is now urgent.

The brands that moved toward first-party data strategies before the regulations tightened are in a structurally different position.

They're not scrambling to assess third-party pixel exposure. They own their data. Their compliance surface is smaller. Their marketing intelligence is better.

Third-party tracking is getting squeezed from every direction — browsers, regulations, enforcement agencies.

First-party data isn't just the smart marketing play in 2026. It's increasingly the only compliant one.
