## Post

AI-native tools under $50/month are losing 77% of their revenue every year.

Not a typo.

Gross revenue retention of 23% means for every $100 a customer paid you in January, you're keeping $23 by December.

---

This is the quiet disaster in the "just ship an AI wrapper and charge $29/month" playbook.

Low price = low friction to cancel.

Low friction to cancel = you need to constantly acquire new customers just to maintain flat revenue.

And new customer acquisition in 2026 is not cheap.

---

Here's what this data actually tells you:

The SaaS pricing decision isn't just a revenue question. It's a retention architecture question.

When you price too low:
- Customers don't feel the loss of canceling
- You attract tire-kickers who are "just trying it out"
- Every churn event is invisible — no real cost to the customer, so no real fight to keep it

When you price at a level that reflects real value:
- Canceling is a decision with stakes
- You attract buyers who actually want the outcome
- The renewal conversation starts from "this is working" not "remind me what this does"

---

The usage-based pricing trend compounds this. Pure consumption models sound fair, but they make revenue wildly unpredictable. You can't plan headcount, CAC, or payback periods on revenue that varies 60% month to month.

The tools winning on retention benchmarks are the ones that solved a specific, painful, measurable problem — and priced accordingly.

23% gross revenue retention isn't a pricing problem.

It's a value proposition problem wearing a pricing costume.

---

Build something people can't afford to lose.

Price it like they mean it.
