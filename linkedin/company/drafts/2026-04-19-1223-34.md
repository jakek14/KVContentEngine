## Post

California's DROP platform goes live August 1, 2026.

One request. Every registered data broker. Every 45 days.

---

Here's what that means for B2B marketing:

Any California resident can systematically remove themselves from every purchased contact list — before you even know they were on it.

The California Privacy Protection Agency has authority to issue $7,500 per intentional violation.

The data broker industry that powers most outbound list-building is now operating inside a strict regulatory window.

---

What survives this?

First-party data.

The contacts who visited your website.
The prospects who engaged with your content directly.
The customers who opted in through channels you actually own.

No deletion request touches a visitor who came to your site today.

---

B2B marketing teams doubling down on purchased outbound lists are building on infrastructure that's actively being regulated away.

The teams building first-party audiences — owned email, known website visitors, CRM contacts from real interactions — are building something that compounds as the environment around them gets more restrictive.

The data landscape tightens every year.

The only audience that reliably survives it is the one you earned.
