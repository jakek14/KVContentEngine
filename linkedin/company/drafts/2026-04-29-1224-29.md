## Post

For the first time in the history of digital advertising, Meta is forecast to surpass Google in total ad revenue.

Meta: $243 billion. Google: $239 billion.

This is a map change.

---

Google built its empire on search intent. Someone types "best CRM for growing teams" or "Salesforce alternatives" — and you show up at the exact moment they're looking. High intent. Lower funnel. Measurable ROI. Clean attribution.

That model still works. For conversion-focused campaigns targeting buyers who are actively searching, Google Search remains one of the most efficient channels available.

But that's not the whole buying journey.

---

Meta's ad business is growing at 24.1% this year. Google's at 11.9%.

That growth rate gap reflects something real: where attention is moving, and where consideration happens.

Short-form video. Creator content. Reels. The default scroll behavior for B2B buyers who aren't searching right now but are absolutely forming opinions about vendors, categories, and pricing.

The buyers who find you on Google often encountered your brand somewhere else first.

That "somewhere else" is increasingly Meta.

---

Most B2B marketing budgets were built around the Google model. The intent signal was cleanest there, so the money followed.

But intent signals don't explain who ends up on your shortlist. Brand familiarity does. Repeated exposure does. The sense that a company exists and matters — built over time, across channels.

That layer — the consideration layer, the mid-funnel — has lived on Meta for years. The revenue data just confirmed it.

---

For B2B companies thinking about where 2026 budget goes: Google is still where you close. Meta is increasingly where you're remembered.

Both matter. The question is whether your current allocation reflects the new map.
