## Post

Google reversed course on cookie deprecation.

Cookies are staying in Chrome. That's the headline everyone ran with.

Here's what actually happened.

---

Google didn't preserve the tracking ecosystem. They killed the Privacy Sandbox — the replacement they spent 10 years and billions building. Topics API, Protected Audience, all of it. Gone.

So cookies stay. But the alternative they were going to replace cookies with didn't survive either.

What you're left with: Chrome keeps third-party cookies, but Safari and Firefox block them by default — and always have. Roughly 30-40% of your traffic was already cookieless before the announcement. That hasn't changed.

---

The lesson isn't "cookies are fine." The lesson is: browser policy was never the right foundation for a data strategy.

Whether cookies survived or not, the brands that built first-party data infrastructure are ahead. Not because they were hedging. Because collecting your own visitor data was always more durable than depending on a signal a browser vendor could revoke overnight.

At KnownVisitors, we built on first-party identification from the start — no cookies required. Because knowing who's on your site shouldn't depend on what Google decides next quarter.

The right question was never "will cookies survive?"

It was: do you own your visitor data, or does the browser?
