## Post

The average DTC brand loses $29 on every new customer they acquire.

CAC is up 222% over the last 8 years. Only 28% of first-time buyers ever come back.

You're not building a business at that rate. You're subsidizing Meta's stock price.

The brands that win aren't spending more on ads. They're extracting more value from the traffic they already paid for.

Stop trying to fill a leaky bucket. Fix the leak first.

## Context

DTC acquisition economics have quietly become catastrophic — most brands are burning money acquiring customers who never return, in a market where ad costs have more than tripled over a decade. This reframe challenges the instinct to "spend more on ads" and pivots to retention and traffic monetization — the exact space KnownVisitors operates in. Chosen because the stats are fresh, shocking, and immediately relatable to any e-commerce or SaaS founder running paid acquisition.
